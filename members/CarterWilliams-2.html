Carter Williams

- Class of 2020

Carter Williams is a game designer and developer who enjoys experimental design, juicy animation and clean code. His favorite games to design are cooperative games that force players to interact in new ways. As a part of making these games, he has developed a networking framework for Unity.

In addition to making games, Carter also has a passion for teaching. He has taught courses on game analysis and game development as well as TA'ing courses on functional programming and educational technology.

Backside Breakout

Fall 2018

Carter Williams

Tomas San Miguel

Adrian Mester

Tiger Jia

Dominic Calkosz

Backside Breakout is a prison break role-playing game. Gameplay is turn based with multiple turns a day before players have to move back to their cells and lose any items not properly hidden. The most convenient place to stash items is in one's rectum, hence the title Backside Breakout. The game is won by fulfilling one of three different escape routes, possibly involving bribery, disguises, or a copious amount of spoons. This game was created at the 2018 Pittsburgh IGDA Board Game Jam, for the theme: Freedom.
